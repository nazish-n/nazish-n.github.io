About Me

I am a fifth-year PhD student at MIT, where I develop low-power, efficient wireless sensing and imaging systems with applications in industrial automation, in-home sensing, and climate monitoring. Specifically, my work has focused on leveraging various sensing modalities inclusing acoustics, vision and RF to build systems that can see the unseen.

I have also had the privilege of conducting research at Microsoft Research and Samsung Research America as a summer research intern. Prior to starting my PhD, I received an SM from MIT and a BS in Electrical Engineering from LUMS.
